# Legilo Privacy Policy

**Effective date: 10 July 2026**

Legilo is a Markdown editor that runs entirely on your device. It was built
around a simple principle: **your documents are yours, and they never leave
your computer.**

## What Legilo does NOT do

- Legilo does **not** collect, store, or transmit any personal data.
- Legilo has **no** telemetry, analytics, advertising, tracking, crash
  reporting, or user accounts.
- Legilo does **not** send your documents, or any information about them,
  anywhere.

## What stays on your device

- **Your documents** are opened and saved only when you choose them, and only
  to locations you choose.
- **App settings** (theme, layout, window size, paper size, the file paths of
  recently open tabs, and the path of an optional custom stylesheet) are
  stored in a local configuration file on your device so the app can restore
  your workspace. This information is never transmitted.
- **Spellchecking** uses your operating system's built-in spellchecker
  (Windows) or a locally stored dictionary (macOS/Linux, downloaded once by
  the underlying Chromium framework). Words you add to the dictionary are
  stored locally.

## Network access

Legilo itself makes no network requests. Two user-initiated exceptions:

- If one of **your documents embeds an image by web address**
  (e.g. `![](https://example.com/image.png)`), the preview loads that image
  from that server, which will see a normal web request from your device.
- **Clicking a link** in the preview opens it in your default web browser.

## Changes

If a future version of Legilo ever changes any of the above, this policy will
be updated before that version is released.
